Welcome to Seoul
South Korea

Seoul, a city and the captial of South Korea, is located in the northeastern part of Korea, along side the Han river. It is the hub of Korea's economic, culture, and polictical life.

The city offers many tourist attractions to global vacationers and travelers. From sophisticated shopping malls to food-centeric night markets to mile high skyscrappers to temples, there are plentiful attractions to fullfill visitor's days.

People enjoy Seoul because of the uncomplicated transportation, delectable food selections, and felictious activities.

One extraordinary feature of Seoul is its straightforward subway system with historical stops along the way. Effortlessly, traverlers are able to explore more of outside of Seoul and enjoy more shopping districts, landmarks, and nightlife. It is a safe city that can be enjoyed by all ages, first timers, group, and solo traverlers to Korea. While visiting Seoul, visit Incehon, Jeju Island, and Busan. All are a quick trip and you get to experience more of Korea and its natural beauty


Popular Attractions in Seoul

  • Myeongdong Market: A premier shopping district with department stores and side shops. Buy the latest K-beauty products, clothes, and jewelery.
  • N Seoul Tower: A modern structure that reaches 237 meters high. See sweeping iconic views of Seoul and enjoy a meal at the cafe.
  • Changdeokgung Palace: One of the remaining palaces in Seoul. Take vibrant and dramantic photos inside at the Huwon garden.
  • Bukchon Hanok Village: Visit hanoks, traditional Korean homes. It is known as one of the most beautiful districts the features guesthouses, restaurants, and teahouses.

Here are the Top 5 Must-Try Foods in Seoul

Korean cuisine is based on rice, meat, and veggies, with traditional meals include side dishes called banchan. Kimchi, one of the most recognized Korean foods, refers to fermented dishes primary made with napa cabbage, radish or cucumber. It is seasoned with gochugaru, garlic, and onions. Seoul has a plethora amount of dishes you can enjoy when visiting.

  1. Kimbap: Rice with meat and veggies, rolled in dried seaweed
  2. Tteokbokki: Spicy rice cakes in a chili sauce
  3. Chimaek: Beer and fried chicken meal
  4. Kalguksu: Knife-cut wheat flour noodles with broth and ingredients
  5. Hotteok: Pancake filled with brown sugar
